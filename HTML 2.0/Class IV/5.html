Biography

Linus Benedict Torvalds is a Finnish software engineer who is the creator and lead developer of the Linux kernel, used by Linux distributions like Debian, Arch and Android. He also created the distributed version control system Git. He was honored, along with Shinya Yamanaka, with the 2012 Millennium Technology Prize by the Technology Academy Finland "in recognition of his creation of a new open source operating system for computers leading to the widely used Linux kernel." He is also the recipient of the 2014 IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award and the 2018 IEEE Masaru Ibuka Consumer Electronics Award.

Achievements

  • Creation of Linux: Linus Torvalds started working on the Linux kernel in 1991.
  • Open Source Philosophy: Linux was released under the GNU General Public License (GPL), promoting open source software principles. This occurred in 1992.
  • Linux Community: Linus began building and nurturing the Linux community in the early 1990s, which continues to grow to this day.
  • Git Version Control System: Linus Torvalds created Git in 2005.
  • Honors and Awards: He received the Millennium Technology Prize in 2012, the IEEE Computer Society Computer Pioneer Award in 2014, and was named one of Time magazine's "100 Most Influential People" in 2017.
  • Continuing Contributions: Linus Torvalds remains active in the development of the Linux kernel, providing ongoing guidance and leadership.
  • Influence on the Open Source Movement: His work on Linux and its development model have influenced the open-source software movement since the early 1990s.
  • Technological Impact: Linux has been a significant player in various fields such as supercomputing, cloud computing, and smartphones. Its impact has continued to grow since its inception in 1991.
